Institue Vision

SCTR's Pune Institute of Computer Technology envisions to be a centre of excellence in higher technical education and research, producing global leaders in the field of technology.

Department Vision

The department endeavors to be recognized globally as a centre of academic excellence and research in Information Technology.

Institue Mission

Provide rigorous academic training that equips students to excel in their professional careers with the development of universal human values. Foster research and innovation culture amongst faculty and students by encouraging interdisciplinary collaborations, thereby contributing to technological advancements. Nurture leadership qualities in students through development of analytical thinking, problem-solving, and effective communication skills.

Department Mission

To inculcate research culture among students by imparting information technology related fundamental knowledge, recent technological trends and ethics to get recognized as globally acceptable and socially responsible professionals

AWS Cloud Ideathon 2024

Date: 20/09/2024
Time: 3:00 pm to 4:30 pm
Resource Person: Jen Looper, Head of Academic Advocacy at AWS, Boston, US

Discovering the Future with Cloud: A Journey of Innovation and Creativity at AWS Cloud Ideathon 2024 in PICT.

The AWS Cloud Ideathon, organized by AWS Cloud Club PICT, was successfully held on 20th September 2024 at Pune Institute of Computer Technology. During the event, students presented their innovative project ideas and competed for exciting prizes.

The Start of the Event

The event began with a captivating session by Jen Looper titled "The Last Saree." She showcased the fascinating intersection of Machine Learning (ML), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and the cultural significance of India’s iconic garment—the saree.

Discussions revolved around how ML techniques could recognize intricate patterns from sarees across regions and eras. Additionally, Generative AI was explored for innovating modern saree designs while honoring tradition.

Jen Looper during her session "The Last Saree"

The Competition

After the session, the Ideathon began, with Jen Looper and Sambit Bhattarai serving as the judges. Six teams presented their projects in the finale, fostering new ideas with cloud computing at AWS Cloud Club PICT.

Winning Teams:

  • Winning Team: AWS Credits worth USD 200 and AWS Swags
  • First Runner-Up: AWS Credits worth USD 75 and AWS Swags
  • Second Runner-Up: AWS Credits worth USD 50 and AWS Swags

Supported by Dr. Archana Ghotkar (Head of IT Department), Rachna Chhajed Karnavat (Associate Professor), and AWS Cloud Club PICT members, the event concluded with inspiration and joyful memories.

Poster Presentation Competition for Third-Year Students

Date: 28/09/2024
Time: 10:00 am to 5:00 pm
Reviewers: Mr. Swarup Godbole (Oracle); Mr. Ashish Mane (BNP Paribas); Mr. Ketan Kadam (HSBC Software); Mr. Vaibhav Tandale (Hexaware Technologies); Mr. Mahesh Yadav (UTS Global)
Co-ordinators: Mr. Sachin Shelke, Mrs. Archana Kadam

Showcasing Innovation: A Thrilling Poster Presentation Competition at PICT Pune.

The IT Department of Pune Institute of Computer Technology (PICT) recently organized a Poster Presentation Competition, where third-year students showcased their innovative ideas and research on topics like Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Blockchain.

Students presenting their posters during the competition

The Competition

The event provided a platform for students to present complex concepts visually. Posters were judged on clarity, originality, rigor, and appeal by an industry panel comprising experts from Oracle, BNP Paribas, HSBC, Hexaware, and UTS Global.

Winners of the Competition

Mr. Shreyas Shirwadkar won first prize for his poster on "Delivery Route Optimization." Mr. Atharva Raut and Ms. Unnati Rathi secured second and third prizes, respectively. Winners received certificates and prizes, with interactive Q&A sessions offering valuable feedback from judges.

Organized by Mr. Sachin Shelke, Mrs. Archana Kadam, and their team, the event was a success under the guidance of Director Dr. P. T. Kulkarni, Principal Dr. S. T. Gandhe, and HoD IT Dr. Archana Ghotkar.

Held annually, this competition encourages students to innovate and contribute to the research landscape.

Debate Competition for SE Students

Date: 19th - 20th September 2024 (4:00 pm to 5:00 pm)
Coordinators: Mrs. D. P. Salapurkar, Mrs. J. H. Jadhav, Mr. V. R. Tribhuvan, Mrs. A. S. Kadam, Ms. A. G. Ghule

Students presenting their posters during the competition

The Department of Information Technology at PICT Pune organized an engaging "Debate Competition" for second-year students on 19th and 20th September 2024. Held under the Soft Skills course, the competition aimed to develop students' critical thinking, communication, and argumentation skills.

A pre-competition registration process was conducted via Google Forms, with 39 students signing up to participate. Based on the registrations, 10 teams were formed, and 6 teams attended the first round held on 19th September 2024. Judges evaluated the participants based on predefined criteria and shortlisted 3 teams for the second and final round, conducted on 20th September 2024.

Objectives of the Debate Competition:

  • Foster critical thinking and analytical abilities.
  • Improve public speaking and persuasive communication skills.
  • Encourage research and evidence-based arguments.
  • Promote teamwork and collaboration in team-based formats.

Students presenting their posters during the competition

The event, meticulously coordinated by Mrs. D. P. Salapurkar and her team, was a platform for students to hone their soft skills, preparing them for future academic and professional endeavors.

Online Expert Session on “Resume Writing” for SE Students

Date: 12th August 2024 (4:00 PM to 5:00 PM)
Organized by: SSL Team, Department of Information Technology, PICT Pune
Speaker: Dr. Tanaya Patil, Career Counsellor and Professional Development Expert
Mode of Conduction: Online

On 12th August 2024, the Department of Information Technology at PICT Pune hosted an engaging online session on "Resume Writing" for second-year students. Conducted by Dr. Tanaya Patil, a seasoned career counsellor, the workshop aimed to equip students with the skills to craft compelling resumes that enhance their employability in today’s competitive job market.

Key Highlights:

  • Showcasing Skills and Competencies: Strategies for effectively presenting personal strengths.
  • Detailing Professional Experience: Tips for structuring and emphasizing internships, projects, and work experience.
  • Highlighting Education: Guidelines for listing academic achievements and certifications.

Students presenting their posters during the competition

The session concluded with an interactive Q&A segment where students voiced their concerns about common challenges in resume writing, such as addressing employment gaps, incorporating internships, and crafting strong objective statements. Dr. Tanaya Patil provided personalized advice and practical solutions, ensuring students left with actionable insights.

The workshop garnered highly positive feedback from attendees, who appreciated the depth of information and actionable tips provided. Students noted a significant boost in their confidence to present their skills, experiences, and achievements effectively to potential employers.

AlgoMania: Unleashing the Algorithmic Frenzy

Date: 10th July 2024 - 31st July 2024
Co-ordinators: Mrs. Sumitra A. Jakhete, Ms. Prajkta Shinde, Mr. Vineet Tribhuvan

Objectives:

  • To build momentum toward achieving greater competitive programming & DSA goals.
  • To find strong core motivation to solve problems daily and progress in the journey of DSA.
  • To be ready for the internship season within one month.
  • To build strong logical and syntactical superiority to compete with peers and work as a team.

Algomania: A month-long July-August TE IT Classes LeetCode competition.

In this competition, students had to solve DSA, DAA, and algorithm problems on the LeetCode platform. Based on their performance, they were allotted points according to the points system. Students competed in groups, and the rules for group formation had been circulated. The points were recorded in an Excel sheet dedicated to each group, and only the group lead was given access to this sheet. Additionally, the DAA faculty provided a few problem statements from LeetCode, related to topics learned in class, which carried points as well.

Algomania is a long-term coding competition developed to test the algorithmic and coding skills of the participants. It serves as a habit-building competition that encourages students to code regularly over a long interval.
